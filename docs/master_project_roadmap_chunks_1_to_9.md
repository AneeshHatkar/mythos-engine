# MythOS Engine Master Roadmap: Chunks 1 to 9

This document locks the updated project order before Chunk 6 begins.

## Why this roadmap lock exists

Chunks 1 to 5 are verified and pushed. Before continuing, the project needs a clean roadmap so new world-simulation, genre, product, legal, ML, frontend, backend, dataset, and deployment work does not overlap or break the existing story generation pipeline.

## Locked Chunk Order

### Chunk 1 — Foundation, Core Schemas, Base Contracts, and Project Skeleton

Purpose:
- Establish backend project structure.
- Define core schemas.
- Create health/root endpoints.
- Create early test structure.
- Establish local workflow, virtual environment, and Git workflow.

Output:
- Stable FastAPI foundation.
- Base contracts for later engines.
- Initial test suite.

### Chunk 2 — Characters, Emotions, Relationships, Agency, and Motivations

Purpose:
- Model characters, goals, emotions, relationships, agency, secrets, choices, and inner drives.
- Prepare characters to interact with worlds, objects, cultures, species, factions, and plot events.

Output:
- Character state foundations.
- Emotional and relationship engines.
- Agency/motivation structures.

### Chunk 3 — World, Factions, Society, Law, Economy, History, and Belief Systems

Purpose:
- Model world-level social structures.
- Include factions, law, economy, history, religion, belief, institutions, power systems, and cultural systems.

Output:
- World and civilization foundations.
- Social/economic/faction context.
- Society-level constraints that influence character behavior.

### Chunk 4 — Plot, Causality, Events, Memory, Consequences, and Narrative State

Purpose:
- Track events, cause/effect, open loops, consequences, memory, and narrative state.
- Make sure changes persist and affect future story generation.

Output:
- Plot and causality engines.
- Memory/state tracking contracts.
- Consequence-aware story state.

### Chunk 5 — Story Generation Pipeline, Drafting, Revision, Export, Benchmarking, and Smoke Testing

Purpose:
- Generate story scenes, chapters, outlines, dialogue, prose, screenplays, series structures, game scenes, revisions, exports, benchmarks, smoke tests, and learning feedback.

Output:
- Story generation pipeline.
- API routes.
- Export store.
- Benchmark pack.
- Smoke test runner.
- Learning feedback adapter.
- Chunk 1 to 5 integration verifier.
- Final Chunk 5 verifier.

### Chunk 6 — Deep World, Ecology, Civilization, Species, Culture, Object, and Life Simulation Expansion

Purpose:
- Expand the world into a living simulation that readers/viewers can fall in love with.
- Add geography, climates, weather, biomes, flora, fauna, species, cities, towns, villages, roads, paths, trade routes, secret places, objects, artifacts, resources, daily life, culture, language, rituals, technology, architecture, food systems, ecology, and environmental consequences.
- Make characters interact with the world as a living system, not a flat backdrop.

Output:
- Deep world expansion schemas.
- Ecology engines.
- Civilization engines.
- Settlement engines.
- Species/flora/fauna engines.
- Object/artifact/resource engines.
- Culture/language/daily-life engines.
- Travel and environmental constraint engines.
- World-state memory hooks for story generation.

### Chunk 7 — Genre Engines, Specialized Story Modes, and Commercial Narrative Patterns

Purpose:
- Add genre-specific engines after the world simulation exists.
- Support fantasy, sci-fi, thriller, mystery, romance, comedy, horror, epic, anime-style arcs, game narratives, cinematic modes, and commercial story patterns.

Output:
- Genre engines.
- Specialized story modes.
- Commercial narrative templates without making the output generic.
- Genre-aware quality gates.

### Chunk 8 — Real-World Adaptation, Casting, Product, Legal, Commercialization, and IP Strategy

Purpose:
- Add adaptation layers for movies, shows, games, books, products, casting influence, audience targeting, legal/IP checks, commercialization, studio workflows, and licensing strategies.

Output:
- Adaptation engine contracts.
- Casting/product/legal/IP strategy modules.
- Commercial packaging plans.
- Ethical and rights-aware workflows.

### Chunk 9 — ML, Data Science, Research, Frontend, Backend, Deployment, Testing, and Full Coding Roadmap

Purpose:
- Turn the system into a research-grade, production-grade platform.
- Add dataset pipelines, model training, model evaluation, embeddings, preference learning, reward modeling, frontend, backend hardening, deployment, observability, experiment tracking, and large-scale testing.

Output:
- ML/data roadmap.
- Training dataset specs.
- Evaluation metrics.
- Research experiments.
- Frontend/backend/deployment plan.
- Full production roadmap.

## Key Placement Decision

Chunk 6 must happen before Chunk 7, Chunk 8, and Chunk 9.

Reason:
The world simulation layer must exist before genre engines, commercial adaptation, and ML training. Genre and ML systems should learn from rich world/character/story structures, not from shallow placeholders.

## Interconnection Rule

All chunks must remain connected through contracts, schemas, memory state, provenance, and verification scripts. No future chunk should become a separate disconnected module.

## Pre-Chunk 6 Rule

Before Chunk 6 begins:
- Chunks 1 to 5 must remain verified.
- Chunk 5 final verification must pass.
- Deep world requirements must be documented.
- Compatibility hooks must be documented.
- The master roadmap must be locked.
